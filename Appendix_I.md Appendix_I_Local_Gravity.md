I.8 Derivations







I.8.1 Derivation of the Effective Local Potential



To show that the local effective potential remains consistent with Newtonian physics, we start from the general form of the gravitational potential with torsion:



\Phi_\text{eff}(\mathbf{r}) = -\frac{G M}{r} + \delta \Phi_\text{torsion}(\mathbf{r})



The torsion correction term is given by:



\delta \Phi_\text{torsion}(\mathbf{r}) \sim \alpha_1 |S_\mu^\text{local}|^2 r^2



Given that the local torsion magnitude is:



|S_\mu^\text{local}| \sim \frac{\ell_\text{Bedrock}}{L_\text{local}} \approx \frac{3 \times 10^{-33}\,\mathrm{m}}{1\,\mathrm{AU}} \approx 3 \times 10^{-44}



And considering that r at local scales (e.g., planetary distances) is on the order of 1\,\mathrm{AU}, we have:



\delta \Phi_\text{torsion} \sim \alpha_1 \times (3 \times 10^{-44})^2 \times (1\,\mathrm{AU})^2



Since 1\,\mathrm{AU} \approx 1.5 \times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{m}, the resulting correction is on the order of:



\delta \Phi_\text{torsion} \sim \alpha_1 \times 9 \times 10^{-88} \times (1.5 \times 10^{11})^2 \approx \alpha_1 \times 2 \times 10^{-65}



Thus, the torsion correction is extremely small and negligible compared to the Newtonian potential -\frac{G M}{r}.








I.8.2 Derivation of Torsion Suppression



To understand why local torsion effects are negligible, we consider the ratio of the Bedrock scale \ell_\text{Bedrock} to the local scale L_\text{local}:



|S_\mu^\text{local}| \sim \frac{\ell_\text{Bedrock}}{L_\text{local}}



With \ell_\text{Bedrock} \approx 3 \times 10^{-33}\,\mathrm{m} and L_\text{local} (e.g., 1\,\mathrm{AU} \approx 1.5 \times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{m}), we have:



|S_\mu^\text{local}| \approx \frac{3 \times 10^{-33}}{1.5 \times 10^{11}} \approx 2 \times 10^{-44}



This extremely small magnitude ensures that any torsion-related effects are Planck-suppressed and therefore negligible at local scales.
