Appendix AG.7 — Torsion Tensor Structure, Contortion, and Cosmological Coupling 










AG.7.1 Scope and Effective Description





This appendix develops an effective field theory (EFT) description of a restricted torsional sector of spacetime. The construction does not attempt to represent the full set of torsion degrees of freedom in general metric-affine gravity. Instead, it isolates a trace-vector torsion subsector sourced by a scalar potential \phi.



All results should be understood as:



Valid within a low-energy, coarse-grained regime
Dependent on a specific torsion ansatz (not the most general case)
Defined up to convention-dependent order-unity normalization factors









AG.7.2 Scalar-Generated Trace Torsion





The torsion tensor is modeled as being generated by gradients of a scalar field \phi, normalized by a fundamental scale \Lambda_0:



S^\lambda_{\ \mu\nu} = \frac{1}{\Lambda_0} \left( \delta^\lambda_{\ \mu} \, \partial_\nu \phi - \delta^\lambda_{\ \nu} \, \partial_\mu \phi \right)



This ansatz:



Is antisymmetric in \mu \leftrightarrow \nu
Represents a restricted trace-vector torsion configuration
Suppresses torsional effects by the scale \Lambda_0
Does not include axial or tensor torsion components




Thus, the scalar field \phi acts as a potential encoding a specific torsional subsector rather than the full torsion manifold.








AG.7.3 Contortion Tensor Relation





The contortion tensor is defined as:

K^\lambda_{\ \mu\nu} = \frac{1}{2} \left( S^\lambda_{\ \mu\nu} - S_{\mu\ \nu}^{\ \lambda} - S_{\nu\ \mu}^{\ \lambda} \right)



Substituting the torsion ansatz yields a contortion tensor linear in derivatives of \phi. All index raising and lowering is performed with the spacetime metric g_{\mu\nu}, and contractions follow standard Einstein summation conventions.








AG.7.4 Torsional Strain Definition





The torsional strain amplitude \epsilon is defined as:

\epsilon \equiv \frac{1}{\Lambda_0} \sqrt{ \left| K^\lambda_{\ \mu\nu} K_\lambda^{\ \mu\nu} \right| }



This definition ensures:



\epsilon is dimensionless
Torsional effects vanish as \Lambda_0 \to \infty
The invariant measures the magnitude of torsional deformation in a coordinate-independent way









AG.7.5 Relation to Field Gradients





For the trace-vector torsion ansatz:

K^\lambda_{\ \mu\nu} K_\lambda^{\ \mu\nu} = c \, (\partial_\mu \phi)(\partial^\mu \phi)



where c is an order-unity coefficient depending on:



torsion normalization conventions
spacetime signature
index placement conventions




In common 4D formulations, c \in \left[\frac{3}{2}, 6\right].



Substituting into the strain definition:

\epsilon = \frac{\sqrt{c}}{\Lambda_0} \sqrt{ \left| (\partial_\mu \phi)(\partial^\mu \phi) \right| }



In the quasi-static cosmological regime, where spatial gradients dominate:

\epsilon \approx \frac{|\nabla \phi|}{\Lambda_0}



(up to order-unity normalization factors absorbed into c).








AG.7.6 Cosmological Embedding





In an FLRW spacetime with scale factor a(t), the scalar field evolves according to the effective equation:



\ddot{\phi} + (3H + \gamma)\dot{\phi} - \frac{1}{a^2}\nabla^2 \phi + \frac{dV}{d\phi} = 0



This assumes:



Canonical kinetic term for \phi
Minimal coupling to curvature
No explicit non-minimal torsion-curvature cross terms at leading order




where:



H = \dot{a}/a is the Hubble parameter
3H represents cosmological damping
\gamma is an effective internal dissipation term
V(\phi) is the torsional potential









AG.7.7 Effective Dissipation and Lattice Complexity





The dissipation coefficient is modeled as:

\gamma \sim \frac{\gamma_0}{N_{\text{roots}}}



where:



\gamma_0 is a microscopic relaxation scale (with units of inverse time)
N_{\text{roots}} characterizes the number of internal excitation channels




This scaling implies that increased lattice complexity distributes energy across more modes, reducing effective macroscopic dissipation.








AG.7.8 Near-Threshold Dynamics





The torsional strain evolves under competing influences:



Cosmological expansion (3H)
Internal dissipation (\gamma)
Potential-driven dynamics (V(\phi))




A near-threshold regime is defined by:

\epsilon_{\text{init}} \approx \epsilon_c



In this regime:



The system lies near a nucleation boundary
Small perturbations determine threshold crossing
The effective nucleation fraction is highly sensitive to normalization choices









AG.7.9 Effective Damping Structure





The combined damping rate is:

\Gamma_{\text{eff}} \sim 3H + \gamma



This represents the joint influence of:



Cosmological expansion
Internal dissipative processes









AG.7.10 Summary of Assumptions





This framework relies on:



A restricted trace-vector torsion ansatz sourced by a scalar field
Effective field theory treatment in a coarse-grained regime
Order-unity ambiguity in normalization constants such as c
Local (Markovian) approximation for dissipation
Phenomenological scaling of dissipation with lattice complexity
